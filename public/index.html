What the levers mean

Five levers, scored 0–1

Each design becomes these scores; the model turns them into a conversion estimate. Reach is a companion factor.
Clarity C
How quickly someone grasps their gap and its link to the product. Raised by: a specific dollar gap, placed near the top, visually tied to Individual Life (a connected/color-matched bar, a "fills the gap" link).
Guidance G
How clearly the design tells you what to do, in order. Raised by: numbered steps, an explicit plan/path, a recommended default amount.
Authority A
Trust and credibility. Raised by: a "Recommended" mark, a rationale (e.g. 10× income), guaranteed-issue / "no health questions," licensing/carrier language.
Payoff Y
The emotional benefit of closing the gap. Raised by: provision language ("your family would receive," "for life"), a big benefit number, a completion/celebration moment.
Friction F
Higher is worse — it subtracts. Raised by: form fields, competing CTAs, required consent. Lowered by: reassurance ("apply in minutes · no commitment").
Reach 0.2–1
The share of sessions that scroll far enough to see the gap card. A top-of-page gap ≈ 1.0; a card buried in an optional list is much lower — it blends the design's persuasion with a "never saw it" baseline.
For reference, current v6 ≈ C .18 / G .12 / A .10 / Y .12 / F .04; the top design V6b-3 ≈ C .82 / G .85 / A .80 / Y .45 / F .10.
How the scoring works

Each design becomes five lever scores (0–1) plus a reach factor. The model combines a design's levers with each segment's traits through a logistic function, then averages over the weighted population. It's calibrated so current v6 = 12% and v6b = +45% — those two anchors never move, so your uploads slot in relative to them.

The rules pre-fill is a rough rubric — the chips under each lever show what it detected. Claude gives a second opinion from the copy and layout. You set the final score; the rules and Claude only suggest. The model math is deterministic; only Claude's opinion varies run to run.

Internal analysis tool. Lever scores are expert-style judgments, not measurements; the reach and rules heuristics are approximate by design and meant to be adjusted. Baseline design vectors are carried from the persona-grid model of record.
